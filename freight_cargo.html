Warehouse & Inventory Management

Warehouse and Inventory Management involve overseeing the storage, tracking, and movement of goods in a warehouse or distribution center. These fields are critical for optimizing supply chain efficiency, reducing costs, and ensuring timely delivery of products. Freshers earn ₹3–8 lakh, mid-level professionals ₹8–15 lakh, and experienced individuals ₹15–30 lakh. Senior roles can exceed ₹50 lakh annually.

Available Roles

Warehouse Manager

Oversee daily operations in the warehouse, including inventory management and staff supervision.

Inventory Control Specialist

Monitor stock levels, track inventory, and ensure accurate record-keeping.

Forklift Operator

Operate forklifts and other material-handling equipment to move goods within the warehouse.

Supply Chain Analyst

Analyze data to improve supply chain processes and optimize inventory levels.

Order Picker/Packer

Pick and pack customer orders efficiently while maintaining accuracy.

Logistics Coordinator

Coordinate shipments, deliveries, and transportation schedules to ensure smooth operations.

Receiving Clerk

Inspect and process incoming shipments, ensuring quality and quantity match purchase orders.

Stock Controller

Manage stock levels, conduct audits, and prevent overstocking or stockouts.

Shipping Supervisor

Oversee the packing and dispatching of goods to ensure timely deliveries.

Procurement Specialist

Source and purchase materials, negotiate contracts, and manage supplier relationships.

Material Handler

Load, unload, and organize materials in the warehouse for efficient access and storage.

Demand Planner

Forecast demand for products and plan inventory levels to meet future needs.
